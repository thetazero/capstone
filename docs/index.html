Finding Eigenvalues of the linearized Navier-Stokes and Euler Equations

Introduction

The program finds solutions to the linearized 2D α-Euler equations on a torus for unidirectional flows. This is done in order to determine the stability of the case.

Cases

The circle has radius p. For case I0, q+p and q-p are out of the circle. For case I+, q+p is on the boundary and q-p is out of the circle. For case I-, q-p is on the boundary and q+p is out of the circle. For case II, either q+p or q-p must be in the circle.

The equations are listed as Euler, Navier-Stokes.

For case I0 the equations are

$$\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{0}}+f(\lambda)+g(\lambda)=0,\quad a_{0}(\lambda, \nu)+f(\lambda, \nu)+g(\lambda, \nu)=0$$

For I+

$$\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{0}}+g(\lambda)=0, \quad a_{0}(\lambda, \nu)+g(\lambda, \nu)=0$$

For I-

$$\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{0}}+f(\lambda)=0, \quad a_{0}(\lambda, \nu)+f(\lambda, \nu)=0$$

For II, things are a little more complicated as the equation should have complex roots, which is the next stage of this project.

α-Euler Case Specifics

$$f(\lambda)=\frac{1}{\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{1}}+\frac{1}{\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{2}}+\dots}}, \quad g(\lambda)=\frac{1}{\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{-1}}+\frac{1}{\frac{\lambda}{\rho_{-2}}+\dots}}$$ $$\rho_{n}=1-\frac{\|p\|^2(1-\alpha^2\|p\|^2)}{\|q+np\|^2(1-\alpha^2\|q+np\|^2)}$$

Navier-Stokes Case Specifics

$$f(\lambda)=\frac{1}{a_{1}(\lambda, \nu)+\frac{1}{a_{2}(\lambda, \nu)+\dots}}, \quad g(\lambda)=\frac{1}{a_{-1}(\lambda, \nu)+\frac{1}{a_{-2}(\lambda, \nu)+\dots}}$$ $$a_{n}(\lambda, \nu)=\frac{\lambda+\nu\|q+np\|^2}{\rho_{n}}$$

Computational Method

In both cases we are dealing with continued fractions. The handy recursive formula for continued fractions allows us to convert the fractions into rational functions. Let \(p_{n}/q_{n}\) be the nth convergent, and \(a_{n}\) be the nth coefficient. Then we have

$$p_{n}=a_{n}p_{n-1}+p_{n-2}$$ $$q_{n}=a_{n}q_{n-1}+q_{n-2}$$

Note that for our case the coefficients $a_{n}$ are first degree polynomials. Given that all coefficients are positive at a certain $\lambda$, this gives us the following error bounds (where x is what the continued fraction converges to)

$$\left |x-\frac{p_{n}}{q_{n}} \right |<\frac{1}{q_{n}q_{n+1}}$$

These rational functions are then computed, along with their corresponding error bounds and plugged into desmos.
